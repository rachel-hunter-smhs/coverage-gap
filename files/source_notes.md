# Source Data Notes

All figures below are drawn from:
"Key Facts about the Uninsured Population" — KFF (Tolbert, Cervantes, Bell, Damico),
published June 16, 2026, analyzing U.S. Census Bureau American Community Survey (ACS) data
for 2023-2024. https://www.kff.org/uninsured/key-facts-about-the-uninsured-population/

Scope: population ages 0-64 (non-elderly), since Medicare provides near-universal
coverage for 65+.

## National headline (2024)
- Uninsured: 26.7 million people, 9.8% uninsured rate (up from 9.5% in 2023, first
  increase since 2019)
- 2019 baseline rate: 10.9%
- Driver: decline in Medicaid coverage (post pandemic-era "unwinding") not fully offset
  by growth in ACA Marketplace / non-group coverage

## State extremes (2024)
- Highest: Texas, 19.2% (nearly double national rate)
- Lowest: Massachusetts, 3.3%
- Non-expansion states (10 states): 14.5% uninsured rate, ~42% of all uninsured
- Medicaid expansion states (41 states + DC): 8.0% uninsured rate, ~58% of all uninsured

## Reasons for being uninsured (adults 18-64, 2024)
| Reason                          | Share citing it |
|------------------------------------|----------------|
| Coverage not affordable              | 61.7% |
| Not eligible for coverage             | 28.9% |
| Did not need/want coverage             | 28.0% |
| Difficulty signing up                   | 21.0% |

## Eligibility for assistance among uninsured (2024)
- Eligible for Medicaid or subsidized Marketplace coverage: 52.9% (14.1 million people)
- Likely ineligible for free/subsidized coverage: 47.1% (12.6 million people)

## Access-to-care gap (2024)
